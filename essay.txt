Apple Inc. was founded on April 1, 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in Cupertino, California. The company started by selling the Apple I computer and quickly became known for innovative designs and intuitive interfaces, shaping the personal computer industry in the decades to come.

Microsoft Corporation was established in 1975 by Bill Gates and Paul Allen in Albuquerque, New Mexico. Initially focused on developing and selling BASIC interpreters for the Altair 8800, Microsoft later grew into a technology giant, dominating the software market with its Windows operating system and Office productivity suite.

Google LLC was founded in September 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were Ph.D. students at Stanford University. The company began as a search engine project and eventually expanded into a wide range of products and services, including advertising technologies, cloud computing, and consumer electronics.

Netflix, Inc. was founded on August 29, 1997 by Reed Hastings and Marc Randolph in Scotts Valley, California. The company originally operated as a DVD rental-by-mail service before transforming into one of the world's leading subscription-based streaming platforms.

Amazon.com, Inc. was founded in July 1994 by Jeff Bezos in Bellevue, Washington. Starting as an online bookstore, Amazon expanded rapidly into various categories, from electronics to groceries, and pioneered e-commerce innovations such as one-click shopping and same-day delivery.

OpenAI was founded in December 2015 by Elon Musk, Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, Ilya Sutskever, John Schulman, and Wojciech Zaremba. The organization focuses on ensuring artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity by conducting research and deploying AI tools responsibly.

SpaceX, officially Space Exploration Technologies Corp., was founded on March 14, 2002 by Elon Musk in Hawthorne, California. The company aims to reduce the cost of space travel and make life multiplanetary, developing rockets such as the Falcon 9 and Starship.

Intel Corporation was founded on July 18, 1968 by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore. Known for creating the world's first commercial microprocessor, Intel has been a leader in semiconductor innovation for decades.

Zoom Video Communications was founded in April 2011 by Eric Yuan. The company became widely known during the COVID-19 pandemic as a leading platform for virtual meetings and remote communication.
